## GUEST ACQUISITION SYSTEM

### 10 Target Guest Profiles

1. **The CISO at a 300-500 Person Company**
   - Why valuable: shows listeners the destination, not just the journey
   - Why they say yes: credibility building, few podcasts serve this specific niche
   - Where to find: ISC2 community, ISACA LinkedIn group, #CISO hashtag on LinkedIn

2. **The IT Manager Who Got Poached to Director at a Fortune 500**
   - Why valuable: validates that the jump is possible, shows what they saw
   - Why they say yes: their story is remarkable and under-told
   - Where to find: LinkedIn "IT Director" + previous title "IT Manager" filter

3. **The Head of IT Staffing at a 50+ Person Agency**
   - Why valuable: provides the "other side of the table" hiring insight
   - Why they say yes: reach into hiring manager audience = client pipeline for them
   - Where to find: LinkedIn Recruiter profiles, Indeed company search for "IT staffing"

4. **The IT Director Who Implemented Zero Trust at an SMB**
   - Why valuable: security transformation story, relevant to most listeners
   - Why they say yes: case study credibility, speaking circuit material
   - Where to find: CompTIA communities, r/netsec moderators, SANS summits

5. **The Vendor Sales Rep Who Left to Work for the Buyer**
   - Why valuable: inside view on vendor tactics, audience loves the reversal
   - Why they say yes: tells their story on their terms, redemption narrative
   - Where to find: LinkedIn "formerly [CrowdStrike/Palo Alto/Cisco] — now [IT Manager]"

6. **The IT Manager at a Startup (Series A/B, 50-150 employees)**
   - Why valuable: faster pace, different tradeoffs, less bureaucracy angle
   - Why they say yes: startup IT is under-covered, they have opinions
   - Where to find: AngelList, YCombinator directory, ProductHunt

7. **The MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider) Owner**
   - Why valuable: security outsourcing from the vendor side — a perspective most IT managers need
   - Why they say yes: direct access to their ideal customer (in-house IT who might refer or outsource)
   - Where to find: ConnectWise/Datto community, CompTIA ChannelCon

8. **The HR Business Partner Who Supports IT Teams**
   - Why valuable: non-obvious perspective — how HR sees IT hiring/firing differently
   - Why they say yes: HR rarely gets on tech podcasts, fresh platform
   - Where to find: SHRM LinkedIn group, HR subreddit moderators

9. **The IT Manager Who Successfully Negotiated a 35%+ Raise**
   - Why valuable: salary negotiation is evergreen content, highly shareable
   - Why they say yes: story worth telling, contributes to the community
   - Where to find: r/ITManagers salary posts, Levels.fyi IT Manager reports

10. **The Cloud Architect Who Rebuilt a Legacy Infrastructure**
    - Why valuable: technical depth + business case combination
    - Why they say yes: architecture decisions have business impact — usually under-told
    - Where to find: AWS re:Invent community, Azure LinkedIn group, GCP developer circles

## 5 HIGH-LEVERAGE GROWTH HACKS

Ranked by Effort (1-5, 5=hardest) vs. Impact (1-5, 5=highest impact)

| Tactic | Effort | Impact | Focus? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Guest-swap compounding | 2 | 5 | ✅ Yes |
| SEO show notes (Google indexing) | 3 | 4 | ✅ Yes |
| Audiogram clips for LinkedIn | 2 | 3 | ✅ Yes |
| Email signature podcast link | 1 | 2 | ✅ Always-on |
| Short-form video (TikTok/Reels repurposing) | 4 | 3 | Later (Month 4+) |

**Guest Swap Execution (Highest ROI):**
1. List 10 shows with overlapping audiences
2. Research 2 episodes of each show
3. Draft 1 personalized swap email per week
4. Goal: 1 confirmed swap per month by Month 3
5. Each swap = ~50-150 new listeners (many will subscribe)

**SEO Show Notes:**
1. Use Descript to auto-transcribe each episode
2. Post as a 1,200-1,800 word blog post with the episode keyword in H1
3. Link to 2-3 other episodes (internal links)
4. Target featured snippet: "What is [topic]" with a direct 40-50 word answer in the first paragraph
5. Each post competes in Google search 6-12 months after posting — cumulative effect

**Audiogram for LinkedIn:**
1. Pull the 30-60 second "money quote" from each episode
2. Create with Headliner.app (free) or Descript's audiogram feature
3. Post as native video to LinkedIn (not a link)
4. Caption: The quote + 1 question for the audience + "Full episode in comments"
5. IT professionals on LinkedIn watch video but don't click out — keep it native
