# Example: "The IT Leader's Edge" Podcast

**Host:** Marcus Rivera, IT Director at Desert Star Hospitality Group, Las Vegas  
**Background:** 18 years in IT, currently managing 12-person team across 3 hotel properties. CISSP certified. Job hunting for Director/VP role while consulting on the side.

---

## Prompt 1 Output: Concept & Positioning

### SHOW CONCEPT STATEMENT
The IT Leader's Edge is a podcast for mid-career IT professionals who manage teams and budgets at companies between 50-500 employees — the people responsible for keeping the lights on AND leading their organizations into an AI-first future. Most IT podcasts either go deep on technical skills or focus on C-suite strategy. This show lives in the gap: the real decisions that IT directors and managers make every week — vendor negotiations, team culture, security without a CISO-level budget, and the career moves that separate the people who stay directors forever from the ones who become VPs and CISOs. Practical, honest, and built for the practitioners who've already earned their stripes.

---

### PODCAST NAME: 5 Candidates

**Option 1: The IT Leader's Edge**
- Why it works: "Edge" signals competitive advantage. Clear audience (IT leaders). Searchable.
- Domain likelihood: Medium — "edge" is common but the full phrase is distinctive
- Category fit: Technology → Tech News

**Option 2: Reboot: IT Leadership**
- Why it works: "Reboot" signals fresh perspective + the IT double-meaning. Professional but playful.
- Domain likelihood: High — "rebootleadership" or "rebootit" likely available
- Category fit: Business → Management

**Option 3: Director Level**
- Why it works: Names the aspiration of the listener (reaching director level OR operating at director level). Exclusive feeling.
- Domain likelihood: High — directolevel.com or directorlevelpodcast.com likely clean
- Category fit: Business → Careers

**Option 4: The 8-Figure IT Manager**
- Why it works: Quantifies the goal — you manage an $8M+ tech budget. Curiosity hook.
- Domain likelihood: High
- Category fit: Business → Management

**Option 5: Under the Hood**
- Why it works: Double meaning (tech + behind the scenes). Casual, insider feel.
- Domain likelihood: Low — likely taken in some form
- Category fit: Technology

**→ Recommendation: Option 1 ("The IT Leader's Edge") — clear, searchable, authority signal**

### TAGLINES

### TARGET LISTENER PROFILE

**Demographics:**
- Age: 32-48
- Title: IT Manager, IT Director, Systems Manager, Infrastructure Manager, IT Operations Manager
- Company size: 50-500 employees (mid-market)
- Industry: Hospitality, healthcare, professional services, manufacturing, retail
- Location: Suburban/secondary markets (not Bay Area) — Las Vegas, Phoenix, Dallas, Nashville

**Psychographics:**
- Values: Competence, reliability, being the person others trust to fix things
- Belief: "I've earned this role through real experience, not just certifications"
- Frustration: Treated as a cost center, not a strategic partner. Has to justify every dollar.
- Aspiration: "I want to be the CTO/CISO someday. I want a seat at the table."
- Self-narrative: "I know more than most people in the room but I don't always get credit for it"

**Media Habits:**
- Already listens to: Darknet Diaries (security stories), Command Line Heroes, Manager Tools
- Gets information from: Reddit (r/sysadmin, r/ITManagers), LinkedIn, Spiceworks Community
- Reading: Gartner reports (for bullet points), SANS newsletters, Harvard Business Review (occasionally)

**Top 3 Pain Points:**
1. Vendor negotiations — constantly oversold, struggling to know when a vendor is BS-ing them
2. Team management — hiring, firing, and motivating people in a field that attracts introverts
3. Career ceiling — feeling stuck at manager level, unsure how to make the jump to Director/VP

**Why They'd Subscribe:**
"This show gives me the conversation I wish I could have with a peer who's further along than me — not theory, not vendor pitches, just real talk about how to lead IT teams and advance."

**This show is NOT for:**
- CTOs and VPs at 1000+ person companies (too advanced, different concerns)
- Developers who want to move into management (not the same journey)
- Consultants — this is for practitioners in-house

### UNIQUE ANGLE

- "Unlike Darknet Diaries, this show isn't about war stories — it's about what to DO when you're facing the same situation at work."
- "Unlike Command Line Heroes, this isn't history — it's your Monday morning."

**Positioning Statement:** "The IT Leader's Edge is the only podcast for mid-market IT managers who want to advance to Director or beyond without giving up their technical edge."

### EPISODE FORMAT RECOMMENDATION

- **Length:** 35-45 minutes
- **Format:** Mixed — solo episodes (Mondays, under 25 min) + interviews (Thursdays, 40-45 min)
- **Structure:**
  - Cold open: Real workplace scenario (not a quote, a situation)
  - Intro: 60-second "here's what we're solving today"
  - Main content: 2-3 segments
  - Rapid fire: 3 quick takeaways
  - CTA: "The one thing to do this week"
- **Release cadence:** Weekly (Thursday main episode, Monday solo "quick take")
- **Production level:** Standard — good USB mic (Shure MV7 or Blue Yeti), Riverside.fm for guests, light editing in Descript

---

**Recurring Segments:**
1. **"Vendor Red Flags"** — 5-min segment calling out specific tactics vendors use to oversell
2. **"The Decision I'd Make Differently"** — guest or host reflects on a real past decision
3. **"Reader/Listener Q"** — answer one community question per episode
4. **"This Week's Hack"** — one productivity or leadership tool, tried and tested, no sponsored content
