## EPISODE 1 FULL OUTLINE

**Pre-intro music bumper (5 seconds)**

**Cold Open (45 seconds):**
"A vendor walked into my conference room three years ago with a $240,000 proposal for email security software. I had done my research. I knew the comparable solution cost $38,000. My CFO was nodding along like this number was reasonable. I had 10 seconds to decide: go along with it, or say something. Here's what I said — and here's the framework that's saved my company over $800,000 in vendor contracts since then."

**Main Intro (70 seconds):**
"Welcome to The IT Leader's Edge. I'm Marcus Rivera — 18 years in IT, currently IT Director at Desert Star Hospitality in Las Vegas, managing infrastructure, security, and a 12-person team across three hotel properties. This show is for IT managers and directors who want the real conversations about leading technical teams — not theory, not vendor-sponsored content, not LinkedIn inspiration posts. Real decisions. Today we're starting with the one that affects every single IT manager every single quarter: vendor negotiations."

**Segment 1 — The Anatomy of an IT Vendor Pitch (10 minutes)**
- How vendors are trained to sell (reference-check calls, urgency tactics, anchor pricing)
- The 3 emotional buttons vendors push: fear (security risk), social proof (your competitor has this), loss aversion (price goes up next quarter)
- Why IT managers lose negotiations before they start: they let the vendor set the agenda
- Key insight: "Every vendor has a second number. Your job is to give them a reason to show it to you."
- Bridge: "Knowing how they think is step one. Here's step two — the questions that change the dynamic."

**Segment 2 — The 5-Rule Negotiation Framework (12 minutes)**
1. Never negotiate on the first meeting — always take a week
2. Get competing quotes before any final meeting (even if you've already decided)
3. The one question that drops price 20%: "What does this look like if we extend the contract by 12 months?"
4. The pause technique: let silence do the work after you counteroffer
5. Document everything in email — vendors are more honest when there's a paper trail
- Real example: The $240,000 email security proposal → negotiated to $41,500
- Key insight: "The vendor's goal is to close before you do research. Your goal is to reverse that timing."
- Bridge: "There's a sixth rule that most IT managers skip because it feels awkward. It's the most important one."

**Segment 3 — Protecting the Relationship After the Negotiation (8 minutes)**
- Why IT managers give back negotiated savings to preserve the relationship (and why this is a mistake)
- The professional close: how to say "we went with someone else" without burning a vendor you might need later
- The relationship reset: how to get back to a good working relationship with a vendor after a hard negotiation
- Key insight: "Vendors respect buyers who know their value. The managers who get the best deals are also the most repeat customers."

**Close (70 seconds):**
"Three things from today: One — vendors are trained to sell. Know their playbook. Two — the first number is never the last number. Always wait a week. Three — competing quotes change the conversation before it starts. One thing to do this week: pull up your three biggest vendor contracts and note when they renew. Write it in your calendar six months before each renewal date. That six-month window is when your leverage is highest — not 30 days before when you're desperate. Link in the show notes to the vendor negotiation template I use — it's free. If this episode was useful, share it with one IT manager who's in a negotiation right now. See you next Thursday."
