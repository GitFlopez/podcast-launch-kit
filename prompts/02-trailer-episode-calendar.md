# Prompt 2 — Trailer Script + First 12 Episodes

## Instructions for Use
Run Prompt 1 first. Paste your show concept, target listener profile, and unique angle into the [BRACKETS] below.

## The Prompt

```
You are a podcast content strategist and scriptwriter. I've already defined my show's positioning. Now I need a trailer script and a 12-episode content calendar that will build an engaged audience from zero.

SHOW NAME: [From Prompt 1 output]
TAGLINE: [From Prompt 1 output]
TARGET LISTENER: [Paste the Target Listener Profile from Prompt 1]
UNIQUE ANGLE: [Paste the Unique Angle Statement from Prompt 1]
EPISODE FORMAT: [Length and format from Prompt 1]
LAUNCH DATE: [Target launch date — e.g., "May 15, 2026"]

MY TOPIC IDEAS FOR FIRST 12 EPISODES:
[List 12 rough topic ideas — these are just seeds, you'll refine them]
1. [Topic 1]
2. [Topic 2]
...

MY HOST STORY: [A 2-3 sentence version of your most compelling professional story or insight — the moment you knew what you know, the failure that taught you everything, or the result that surprised even you]

---

Generate the following:

**SECTION 1: TRAILER SCRIPT**

Write two versions of the trailer:

**Version A — Full Trailer (90-120 seconds when read aloud at conversational pace)**
Structure:
- Hook (first 10 seconds): Start with a provocative question, statistic, or bold statement that stops the scroll
- The Problem (10-15 seconds): Name the specific pain your target listener faces daily
- The Promise (15-20 seconds): What this show will give them
- Host credibility (15-20 seconds): Brief, specific, results-focused bio — not a resume
- Who it's for (10 seconds): Call out your listener by name/role/situation
- The invitation (final 15 seconds): Clear CTA to subscribe + hint at upcoming episodes

Format as a script with [PAUSE] markers, [EMPHASIS] markers, and approximate timing per section.

**Version B — 30-Second Teaser Cut**
The trailer distilled to its most powerful 30 seconds. Designed for Instagram/TikTok/Reels pre-roll or paid ads. Starts with the hook, ends with a CTA. No host bio.

**SECTION 2: 12-EPISODE CONTENT CALENDAR**

For each of the 12 episodes, provide:

**Episode [N]: [Working Title]**
- Title Option A: [SEO-optimized, keyword-forward]
- Title Option B: [Curiosity/benefit-driven, more conversational]
- Description (3 sentences): What the episode covers, the key insight or story, and what listeners will take away
- Listener Promise: Complete this sentence: "After this episode, you will [specific outcome]"
- Format: [Interview — describe ideal guest type / Solo — key sections / Panel — who and why]
- SEO Keyword Target: [Primary search term this episode ranks for in show notes]
- Tease at end of previous episode: [One sentence hook to use as a teaser in Episode N-1]

Organize the 12 episodes strategically:
- Episodes 1-3: "Greatest hits" — your most confident, proven topics that showcase your expertise immediately
- Episodes 4-6: Deep dives — longer, richer episodes that build authority
- Episodes 7-9: Perspective variety — guest interviews or counterintuitive angles
- Episodes 10-12: Community and future — listener questions, predictions, forward-looking

**SECTION 3: FIRST 3 FULL EPISODE OUTLINES**

For Episodes 1, 2, and 3, write complete outlines:

**Episode [N] Full Outline:**

*Pre-intro music bumper (5 seconds)*

**Cold Open (30-60 seconds):**
[The hook — start in the middle of a story, with a striking fact, or with a question]

**Main Intro (60-90 seconds):**
- Show introduction for new listeners
- Episode topic framing
- What the listener will walk away with

**Segment 1 — [Segment Name] (X minutes):**
[Bullet outline of key points, stories, examples to cover]
Key insight: [The one thing they must remember]
Bridge to Segment 2: [Transition sentence]

**Segment 2 — [Segment Name] (X minutes):**
[Bullet outline]
Key insight: [The one thing they must remember]
Bridge to Segment 3: [Transition sentence]

**Segment 3 — [Segment Name] (X minutes):**
[Bullet outline]
Key insight: [The one thing they must remember]

**Close (60-90 seconds):**
- Summary: 3 things they learned
- Action step: One specific thing to do this week
- Community CTA: Leave a review / Share with someone / DM your question
- Next episode tease: [One sentence]

**SECTION 4: RECURRING SEGMENT IDEAS**

Suggest 4 recurring segments that create listener habit and make the show feel consistent. For each:
- Segment name
- Format (length, structure)
- Why it works (what psychological hook keeps listeners coming back)
- Example: what it would look like in Episode 1

Good recurring segments feel like a feature, not filler. Examples: "One Tool I Used This Week," "Listener Question," "Common Mistake Monday," "Number of the Week."
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be honest in HOST STORY — the best hooks come from real moments of failure or surprise
- The 12 topic ideas are seeds — the AI will improve them significantly
- "Listener Promise" is the most important field — write it before recording, read it when editing
- Record your trailer last (after you've done 2-3 episodes and found your voice)
