# Prompt 4 — Growth Playbook + Monetization Roadmap

## Instructions for Use
Run Prompts 1-3 first or have your show info ready. This prompt generates your 12-month growth and revenue plan.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a podcast growth consultant who specializes in turning niche shows into profitable media businesses. I've launched my podcast and now need a systematic plan to grow the audience and generate revenue.

SHOW NAME: [From Prompt 1]
TAGLINE: [From Prompt 1]
TARGET LISTENER: [From Prompt 1]
LAUNCH DATE: [When you launched]
CURRENT METRICS (honest answers):
  - Downloads per episode (Week 1-4 average): [e.g., "47 per episode"]
  - Email list size: [e.g., "380 subscribers"]
  - LinkedIn followers: [e.g., "1,200"]
  - Instagram followers: [e.g., "340"]
  - Reviews on Apple Podcasts: [e.g., "3"]
12-MONTH DOWNLOAD GOAL: [e.g., "500 downloads per episode by Month 12"]
MONETIZATION GOAL: [e.g., "$2,000/month by Month 9 from sponsorships + DFY consulting clients"]
HOST OFFER (if any): [Any paid offer you have now or plan to have — e.g., "IT security consulting, $5K+ projects"]
INDUSTRY/NICHE: [e.g., "IT leadership / SMB cybersecurity"]

---

Generate my complete growth and monetization playbook:

**SECTION 1: GUEST ACQUISITION SYSTEM**

Build a systematic guest pipeline for the next 90 days.

**10 Target Guest Profiles (not specific names — ideal guest archetypes):**
For each profile:
- Role/title/position
- Why they're valuable to my listener (social proof, perspective, expertise)
- Why they'd say yes to my show (what's in it for them)
- Where to find them (LinkedIn search string, Twitter/X communities, specific Slack groups, industry newsletters)

**Guest Outreach Pitch Template:**
Write a cold outreach email that:
- Opens with a specific compliment (not generic)
- Establishes show credibility in 2 sentences
- Makes a clear, low-friction ask
- Total length: under 150 words
Write 3 versions: email, LinkedIn DM, Twitter/X DM.

**Guest Intake Process:**
- Pre-interview questionnaire (10 questions that give you everything for the intro + episode outline)
- Pre-recording instructions (tech setup, what to expect)
- Post-recording thank you email template

**SECTION 2: CROSS-PROMOTION STRATEGY**

**5 Shows to Pitch for Swap Episodes:**
For each show:
- Show type/description (real or hypothetical)
- Overlap with my audience (why the swap makes sense)
- What I bring to their show (my topic angle for their audience)
- What they bring to mine (their topic angle for my audience)
- Swap pitch script (email format, under 200 words)

**Bonus: Guest Hosting Pitch**
Template for pitching yourself as a guest on 10+ shows per month (the fastest growth strategy for new podcasts). Include the subject line that gets opens in crowded inboxes.

**SECTION 3: LISTENER CONVERSION FUNNEL**

Map the path from first-time listener → email subscriber → paying customer.

**Step 1 — Lead Magnet for Podcast Listeners:**
- Recommend the highest-converting lead magnet type for my niche
- Write the CTA to use in every episode: [specific words, not a template]
- Landing page above-the-fold copy (headline + subheadline + button text)
- Thank-you page: 2-sentence confirmation + immediate value delivery

**Step 2 — Email Welcome Sequence (5 emails):**
For each email: subject line, preview text, and body outline (key messages + CTA)
- Email 1 (immediate): Deliver lead magnet + set expectations
- Email 2 (Day 2): Your story / why you started this podcast
- Email 3 (Day 4): Best episode recommendation + listener win story
- Email 4 (Day 7): The most common mistake in [niche] + your solution
- Email 5 (Day 10): Soft offer introduction / what you do beyond the podcast

**Step 3 — Listener-to-Client Conversion:**
For show hosts who offer services/consulting:
- The "listener to lead" moment: when and how to mention your offer without sounding salesy
- Episode outro script for service providers (30-second version, not preachy)
- The podcast-to-consultation email: send to new subscribers who match your ICP

**SECTION 4: SEO STRATEGY FOR SHOW NOTES**

Build organic discovery through Google (most podcasters ignore this).

**10 Show Notes Blog Posts Ranked by SEO Opportunity:**
For each post:
- Title (H1): [Exact title for the blog post version of this episode]
- Target keyword: [Primary search term]
- Monthly search volume: [Estimated — Low (<500) / Medium (500-5K) / High (5K+)]
- Competition level: [Low / Medium / High]
- SEO strategy: What makes this post rank (featured snippet? Listicle? Guide?)
- Episode connection: Which of your 12 episodes this expands

**Show Notes Template:**
Write a reusable show notes structure that:
- Targets the episode's SEO keyword
- Includes a transcript summary (Google indexes this)
- Has internal links to 2-3 other episodes
- Ends with an email list CTA

**SECTION 5: GROWTH MILESTONES + LEADING INDICATORS**

**Month 3-6-12 Targets:**
| Metric | Month 3 | Month 6 | Month 12 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Downloads per episode | | | |
| Total subscribers (email) | | | |
| Apple Podcasts reviews | | | |
| Guest count | | | |
| Cross-promotion swaps | | | |
| Monthly revenue | | | |

Fill in realistic targets based on my current numbers and 12-month goal. Mark each target as Stretch (20% chance) / Likely (60% chance) / Conservative (90% chance).

**5 Weekly Leading Indicators to Track:**
(Metrics that predict download growth before you see it in the numbers)

**SECTION 6: MONETIZATION LADDER**

Build a complete revenue roadmap from zero to sustainable income.

**Phase 1 (Episodes 1-20, Months 1-5): Audience Building**
- Revenue: $0 intentionally (build trust first)
- Action: Launch lead magnet, collect emails, get first 10 reviews
- Minimum viable threshold to move to Phase 2: [specific number]

**Phase 2 (Episodes 21-40, Months 5-8): First Revenue**
- Affiliate partnerships: 3 recommended affiliate programs for my niche
  - Product/service
  - Commission structure
  - How to integrate naturally into episodes
  - Monthly revenue potential at [my current listener count]
- Paid community (if applicable): pricing, platform, founding member offer
- One-time DFY offer: what to offer, price point, how to sell it via the podcast

**Phase 3 (Episodes 41+, Month 9+): Sponsorship + Scale**
- **Sponsorship Rate Card:**
  | Placement | Length | Downloads/Episode | Rate |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Pre-roll | 15-30 sec | 1,000 | $[CPM formula] |
  | Mid-roll | 60 sec | 1,000 | $[CPM formula] |
  | Post-roll | 15 sec | 1,000 | $[CPM formula] |
  | Sponsored episode | Full episode | 1,000 | $[CPM formula] |
  
  Fill in at 500, 1,000, 5,000, and 10,000 downloads/episode using industry CPM rates.

- **Sponsor Outreach Pitch:** Cold email template for approaching direct sponsors (non-network)
- **Minimum Viable Audience:** What download threshold makes you attractive to sponsors in your niche

**Revenue Projections:**
| Milestone | Downloads/ep | Monthly Revenue | Primary Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starting point | [current] | $0 | — |
| Phase 2 entry | 300 | $[X] | Affiliate |
| Phase 3 entry | 1,000 | $[X] | Sponsorship + affiliate |
| Scale | 5,000 | $[X] | Sponsorship + DFY |
| Top 1% of shows | 10,000+ | $[X] | Media brand |

**SECTION 7: 5 HIGH-LEVERAGE GROWTH HACKS**

Rank by Effort (1-5) vs. Impact (1-5). Focus on the top-right quadrant.

For each tactic:
- Name
- Effort: [1-5]
- Impact: [1-5]
- How to execute (step-by-step, under 100 words)
- Time to first result (days/weeks)
- Example: What this looks like for my specific show

Tactics to consider: repurposing to short-form video, audiogram strategy for social, SEO show notes, guest-swap compounding, email signature podcast link, community cross-posting, speaking at industry events, featuring listeners, podcast award submissions, re-releasing top episodes with new framing.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be honest about current metrics — overestimating downloads leads to unrealistic targets
- The guest swap strategy (Section 2) is the single highest-ROI tactic for shows under 1,000 downloads/episode
- Don't skip the email funnel — downloads don't pay bills, email subscribers do
- Run this prompt at launch, at Month 3, and at Month 6 — update metrics each time
