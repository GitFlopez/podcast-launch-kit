# Prompt 1 — Podcast Concept & Positioning (FREE)

## Instructions for Use
Copy the prompt below into Claude (claude.ai) or any Claude API integration. Fill in the [BRACKETS] with your information. The output is ready to use.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a podcast strategy consultant who has launched 50+ shows across business, technology, health, and lifestyle categories. Your job is to help me define a podcast that stands out in a crowded market and builds a loyal listener base.

Here's my information:

HOST EXPERTISE: [Your professional background, years of experience, specific niche — e.g., "15 years in IT security, currently IT Director at a Las Vegas hotel company, specializing in zero-trust architecture and team leadership"]

TARGET LISTENER: [Who you want to attract — e.g., "IT managers and directors at mid-size companies (50-500 employees), 35-50 years old, dealing with security threats, team management, and digital transformation"]

TOPIC AREAS: [3-5 broad topics you want to cover — e.g., "Cybersecurity fundamentals, IT leadership, remote team management, vendor negotiations, career growth for IT professionals"]

COMPETING SHOWS: [3 podcasts in your space — e.g., "Darknet Diaries, The CyberWire Daily, Smashing Security"]

EPISODE LENGTH PREFERENCE: [How long you want episodes — e.g., "30-45 minutes, interview format"]

LAUNCH GOAL: [What you want to achieve in Year 1 — e.g., "Build personal brand for consulting clients, position for speaking engagements, eventually monetize with sponsorships"]

---

Based on this information, generate my complete podcast concept and positioning package:

**1. SHOW CONCEPT STATEMENT (1 paragraph)**
Write a 3-5 sentence concept statement that captures: who this show is for, what problem it solves, what makes it different, and what listeners will gain. This is the internal north star — not the public description.

**2. PODCAST NAME (5 Candidates)**
Generate 5 podcast name options. For each, provide:
- Name
- Why it works (2 sentences)
- Potential domain: [name].com or [name]podcast.com availability likelihood (High/Medium/Low based on name uniqueness)
- Category fit: which Apple Podcasts category this name signals

Name types to include: one authority name, one curiosity-driven name, one listener-benefit name, one personality-driven name, one industry-insider name.

**3. TAGLINE (3 Options)**
Each tagline must be under 10 words. Format:
- Option A: [tagline] — [what positioning this communicates]
- Option B: [tagline] — [what positioning this communicates]
- Option C: [tagline] — [what positioning this communicates]

**4. TARGET LISTENER PROFILE**
- Demographics: age range, job title, company size, location (if relevant)
- Psychographics: values, beliefs, frustrations, aspirations
- Media habits: where they currently get information, podcasts they likely already listen to
- Top 3 pain points: specific problems they face that this show addresses
- Why they'd subscribe: what promise keeps them coming back
- "This show is NOT for": define who to exclude (sharpens positioning)

**5. UNIQUE ANGLE STATEMENT**
Complete this sentence in 2-3 ways: "Unlike [Competitor], this show [unique differentiator]."
Then write a one-sentence positioning statement in the format: "[Show Name] is the only podcast for [specific listener] who want [specific outcome] without [common alternative/frustration]."

**6. APPLE PODCASTS + SPOTIFY CATEGORY RECOMMENDATION**
- Primary category
- Secondary category
- Keyword strategy: 5 search terms your target listener would use to discover shows like yours

**7. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE ANALYSIS**
For each of the 3 competing shows I listed, provide:
- Positioning: what they do well
- Gap: what they miss or under-serve
- Your opportunity: how you specifically address that gap

Then plot all 3 shows plus my proposed show on a 2×2 matrix:
- X-axis: Beginner-Friendly ← → Expert-Level
- Y-axis: Tactical/Practical ← → Strategic/Conceptual
Show where each competitor falls and where my show should position.

**8. EPISODE FORMAT RECOMMENDATION**
Recommend the optimal format based on my goals:
- Episode length: [X] minutes. Why: [reason based on listener habits in my category]
- Structure: [Interview / Solo / Panel / Mixed] — explain the tradeoff
- Segments: 3-4 recurring segment ideas that create listener habit and make episodes feel consistent
- Release cadence: Weekly / Bi-weekly / Daily — with rationale
- Production level: Minimal (just a mic) / Standard (light editing) / Premium (full production) — what's right for my goals
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be specific in HOST EXPERTISE — vague input produces vague output
- List real competing shows, not just genres
- The "not for" section is often the most valuable output — it sharpens your audience definition
- Run this prompt before any other — the output feeds Prompts 2, 3, and 4

---

## What to Do With the Output

1. Pick your show name (Candidate 2-3 usually wins — the first is often too obvious)
2. Lock your tagline and use it everywhere (social bio, email signature, listing copy)
3. Save the Target Listener Profile — paste it at the top of every future prompt in this skill
4. Screenshot the competitive matrix — it becomes your positioning slide if you ever pitch sponsors
